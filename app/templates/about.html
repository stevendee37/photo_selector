How it Started

Photography has always been my passion, but as a photographer, I often found myself overwhelmed by the sheer volume of photos I needed to sort through after a shoot. It was a time-consuming process that drained my energy and creativity. In response to this challenge, I embarked on a journey to develop a web app that would revolutionize my photography workflow. My goal was clear: to save time, increase productivity, and, most importantly, allow me to focus more on the art of photography

How it Works

To approach this issue, I turned to machine learning and computer vision techniques. To detect blurriness, I turned to using the Laplacian of the image. By taking the variance of the Laplacian of the image, I was able to come up with a metric to show if a photo was blurry or not. To learn a photos aesthetic value, I leveraged the abilities of the Represenation Learning of Image Composition (ReLIC) framework. The framework, which utilizes MobileNetV2 to extract features and a fully-connected graph to learn, was the foundation for my app's ability to analyze images.

How it's Been Going

This approach not only streamlined the culling process but also enhanced the quality of my selected images. It was a significant breakthrough in my photography workflow, and it's incredibly rewarding to see how these computer vision techniques have made a positive impact on my artistry and productivity.
